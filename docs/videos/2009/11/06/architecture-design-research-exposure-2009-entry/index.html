Architecture Design Research - entry by David Lau (School of Architecture and Planning) for the multimedia category of Exposure 2009 Postgraduate Exposition at The University of Auckland.

ARCHITECTURE DESIGN RESEARCH: DECAY OF THE MIDCITY COMPLEX AND REVIVAL THROUGH ALTERNATIVE URBAN DESIGN STRATEGIES

In the 1980s, the Midcity Development on Queen St was proclaimed as the rebirth of Aucklands heart of entertainment and became our first flagship cinema multiplex. However, if we fast forward to the present day, the Midcity complex is seen as an abandoned and dysfunctional wasteland. My research investigates the cause and reason of this urban decay within the city, as well as seeking an alternative vision based upon successful modern urban strategies that will adapt to the citys constant rate of growth and expansion in the populated mid Queen St location, while creating opportunities for retail and commercial growth. My method of approach involved detail site analysis of the wider urban context of mid-town Auckland City. This process involved conducting investigation through physical mass modelling to help understand the mass, bulk and location while using digital modelling and fabrication through Laser cutting to explore alternative schemes that rejects typical retail proposals. The result is a design which revives the Midcity location and retains the potential connection between Queen Street and Elliott Street while incorporating elements of green public/private spaces, vertical continuation and offers social and entertainment opportunities. The revival design of the Midcity site would serve as a potential catalyst and exemplar to future built design revivals in other deceased and uninteresting sites throughout urban city of Auckland.
