Description

MBChB graduate and RRAS student Ben Alsop-ten Hove grew up in rural Canterbury and had always planned to study at a South Island university. How then did Ben end up studying medicine at the University of Auckland and what does he have to say about his experience in the Big Smoke?

The University of Auckland’s Regional Rural Admission Scheme (RRAS) supports the entrance of more rural people into health care professions. This is incredibly important to not only ensure our medical and health workforces are representative of our society; but also because rural people are more likely to go on to serve rural communities.
