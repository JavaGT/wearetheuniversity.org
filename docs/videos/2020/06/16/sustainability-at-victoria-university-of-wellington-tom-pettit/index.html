At Victoria University I studied Master of Public Policy.
Coming from an overseas undergrad of Management and Business.
My current job supports a sustainable future because we're not just measuring
how much carbon the city is emitting, but we're also running trials and supporting
low-carbon businesses that will help us reduce our overall carbon impacts as a city.
I think the thing I gained most from my time in Victoria is confidence.
I was coming from the private sector, I didn't know if I could operate in
government and by going through a Master of Public Policy, I really gained
the confidence that I could operate in the government sector.
I think the one piece of advice I would offer most, as someone interested in sustainability is,
don't be afraid to go and work for the organisation you want to work for
and slowly move into a sustainability role over time.
I initially started at the City Council working in the research team and it took
me several years to get into a sustainability role.
I'm most proud of biking to work.
In the last four years working in the City Council I've only driven to work twice
and I take a lot of pleasure in getting the wind in my, well, I would say the wind
in my hair, but having the wind on my head.
