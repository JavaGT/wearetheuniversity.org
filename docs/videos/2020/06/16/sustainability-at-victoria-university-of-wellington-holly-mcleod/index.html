The aspect of sustainability that I'm most interested in, is sustainable development.
So sustainable development is the ability of present generations to meet their own
needs, without compromising the ability of future generations to meet theirs as well.
It's really about tying together these aims to achieve human development goals
and alleviate poverty and recognising the need to protect the environment, whilst
trying to create a sustainable future where people can have healthy and fulfilled lives.
Throughout undergraduate levels, we're really focused on understanding the
history of development, understanding what went right, what didn't, and why it
probably hasn't been as successful as the world expected.
And then it seems like for the rest of our degrees and our academic careers,
we head into asking what sustainable development is, what we want it to look like,
how can we achieve it and above all how are we going to make sure it lasts.
So, for me, that idea of sustainable development is really at the moral core of Development Studies.
At the time of apply for study, Victoria was the only university that offered
Development Studies as an option.
So to me that indicated that I was part of a university that was forward thinking,
that was globally minded and that was committed to a global agenda.
Victoria offers a lot of networking opportunities, so in my undergraduate studies,
I participated in VILP, which is the International Leadership Programme.
They offer a lot of speaker and seminar events for you to take part in.
One sustainable action I do everyday, my daily life that what I'm most proud of is walking everywhere.
So I walk to university; I walk to work; I walk to get my groceries; I walk everywhere in between.
Reducing emissions is a really important part of becoming a sustainable city.
So, I do use the public transport system, but mostly I'm walking.
It's good for me, it's good for the environment, it's a win-win situation.
