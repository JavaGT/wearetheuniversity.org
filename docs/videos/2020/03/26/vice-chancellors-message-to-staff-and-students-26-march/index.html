Colleagues and students of the University
of Waikato.
I'm recording this message on Wednesday evening,
the beginning of the Alert Level 4 lockdown,
and the beginning of my four weeks of working
from here in my home office.
The University of Waikato was well prepared
for a move to online teaching and learning
as a response to the COVID-19 virus.
However, we decided to take this week to ensure
a quality transition to online teaching and learning.
So following the Prime Minister's decisions
at the beginning of this week, that we would
move to Alert Level 4, I was, in turn, required
to make some decisions that are quite unprecedented
in the history of this University.
Those decisions included the suspension of
all face-to-face teaching from last Monday
the 23rd of March.
As you're aware, this week only NET papers,
normally taught online, have continued,
and assignments due for other papers, have
been rescheduled.
I announced that we would resume teaching
next Monday 30 March, with all papers taught
in online mode from that point onwards.
I announced that we would be using one week
of the mid-semester break, from the 20th to
24th of April, as a teaching period.
And I announced that there would be no examinations
for A Trimester papers, with the assessment
associated with currently scheduled examinations
being transferred to other pieces of work.
I have committed the University of Waikato
to ensuring that all teaching, learning, and
assessment for A Trimester papers will be
completed by the end of June (2020).
All students can be assured that even if there
are some glitches next week, as we move every
paper into full online mode, the academic
staff of the University will show flexibility
in helping to ensure that you can complete
all of the required learning and all of the
required assignments for your papers by the
end of the trimester.
I've also committed the University to supporting
all of our current staff, irrespective of
their ability to undertake work from home.
I am aware that the staff of the University
have carried a huge load in the past two weeks
as we have dealt with COVID-19 disruption
and the move to an online teaching environment.
I have in fact been quite overwhelmed by the
commitment and the pragmatism that you, my
colleagues, have shown in ensuring that we
could shift to an online delivery mode and
be ready to do it by next Monday the 30th
of March.
I've also been extraordinarily impressed by
the calm and pragmatic way in which our students
have dealt with the disruption of this week.
Not only have classes been disrupted, but
many of you I know have had to make decisions
about whether to move home, to stay in your
flats or in University Halls here to complete
the trimester.
Thank you to all of the students and all of
the staff who've been so fantastic in responding
to the events of this week.
As you're all aware, these are unprecedented
events, and we're operating in unprecedented
times.
And I don't know how long that situation will
continue.
But I do know that what is really important
is that the University Community continues
to come together.
That the communication, the engagement, the
whanaungatanga of the University of Waikato
community, is continued through the adversity
that we currently face.
And I can assure you, that as the government
makes new decisions, we will communicate with
you as quickly as we possibly can about the
implications of those decisions for the University
and also the way in which we will be responding
to those decisions.
Colleagues and students of the University
of Waikato, my very best wishes to you, your
families and friends, for your safety and
good health over the next coming months.
We will all get through this together.
