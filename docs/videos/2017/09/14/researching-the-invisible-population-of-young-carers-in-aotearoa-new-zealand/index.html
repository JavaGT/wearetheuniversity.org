Description

Young carers are children and young adults who take on a significant and on-going caring role for those (often family members) with disabilities, illness, substance misuse, mental illness or who are elderly. Lauren Donnan was a young carer growing up, and is now researching into this often invisible group of people. Her PhD examines the lived experiences of NZ young carers, and aims to use young carers’ own understandings of theirs’ and their families’ needs to expand and inform service provision and policy in NZ.
