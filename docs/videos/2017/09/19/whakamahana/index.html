Whakamahana [5:03]

Watch on Youtube

Creative Arts and Industries, the University of Auckland

Description

Whakamahana was choreographed in 2014 by Teuila Hughes and Mattie Hamuera (both Bachelor of Dance Studies graduates) and was performed by third year students in Macau and for Action, the Dance Studies end of year showcase.

Whakamahana, meaning ‘to warm’, is the celebration of the sense of euphoria that we bring to the stage through our dance. This sensation transcends us into the space beyond, bringing warmth to the performers, the atmosphere and audience.

Music: Whakamahana – Mattie Hamuera/Pukepuke te Pate - Toti's Tahitians
Staging and rehearsal direction: Sarah Knox
