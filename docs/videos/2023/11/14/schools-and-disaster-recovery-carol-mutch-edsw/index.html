After experiencing the Canterbury
earthquakes I began researching what
schools did over and above their usual
educational roles to support their
communities since then I've expanded my
research to cover other disasters in New
Zealand and around the Asia Pacific
including the impact of Co 19 and
Cyclone Gabrielle when disaster strikes
schools become community hubs and
principes become crisis managers
teachers become First Responders and
with their students play an active role
in their own and their community's
recovery most importantly my work has
increased National and international
recognition of the role that schools
play in Disaster Response and
Recovery
