Description

Here in New Zealand, we are particularly proud of our network of protected areas. They bring in millions of international and national visitors each year, but they were established decades ago and we have to ask if they are still fit for purpose.

Te Herenga Waka—Victoria University of Wellington’s Andrew Wilks, Director of Sustainability, sits down with Associate Professor Valentina Dinica to discuss the role of protected areas in New Zealand, what they mean for our ecosystems, and the origins of our national parks and nature reserves. Are we doing the right things to protect these areas? They also visit ZEALANDIA to look into how we can be making more of an effort to preserve a wider range of ecosystems in New Zealand.
