Description

Founded in 1907, Te Whare Wananga o Ōtākou, Te Kaupeka Pūniho, the University of Otago’s Faculty of Dentistry was an early pioneer of dental education and training. Today it remains the only Dental School in Aotearoa New Zealand, and one of the top-50 dental teaching hospitals in the world. While the Faculty has a long and remarkable history in education, research and service to the community, over the last few years the Faculty has undergone significant change. The modern, purpose-built facility now houses state-of-the-art dental clinics, operating theatres, teaching and research laboratories, seminar rooms, a lecture theatre, student areas and offices. The University of Otago’s Faculty of Dentistry continues to lead the world in the provision of exceptional education, teaching and research opportunities, while giving the community access to high-quality dental care. www.otago.ac.nz/dentistry

dental technology, oral health and dentistry.

the Central Sterile Services Department,

sterilised and then transported in dedicated
lifts to the dispensaries on each floor.
At peak times the CSSD cleans and sterilises more
pieces of equipment than Dunedin Public Hospital.
The first floor houses the primary care unit

with acute pain, infections and trauma

equipped operating theater and three procedure

unit houses equipment for bitewing, panoramic
and cone beam computer tomography 3D imaging.
6 dental chairs are dedicated to the clinical
care of children and young adults, as well as
those with special needs. They provide privacy,
less distractions and more space for whānau.
The twelve chairs in orthodontics replicate
typical orthodontic practice, helping to produce
oral health practitioners with a career-ready
clinical skill set. The dental technology labs

dental technology students. Postgraduate
students, particularly prosthodontics,
esthetic dentistry and clinical dental technology

CAD cam design and the milling and 3D printing

The second and third floors of The Clinical
Services Building house the main teaching clinics
for undergraduate and postgraduate students.
Each floor contains 80 dental chairs. Arranged
in pods of eight chairs, the layout enables
patient privacy and discrete supervision,
with a dental tutor overseeing each pod. Six
individual operatories per floor are also
available for more complex procedures. The top
of the range dental chairs are fully digitised so

The second floor of the Walsh Building contains

oral health students to learn clinical procedures

The setup replicates a real-life clinical setting,
preparing students for the treatment of patients.

The Oral Pathology Center provides oral
histopathology diagnostic services to
general dentists, dental specialists and general

The Sir John Walsh Research Institute comprises a
physical containment level 2 molecular biosciences

Located in Central Dunedin, this world-class
