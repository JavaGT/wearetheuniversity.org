This video is part of a series celebrating our partnership with Te Matatini National Kapa Haka Festival. Kaiārataki o te tari o PVC Māori, Michael Steedman explains the seven categories that all groups compete in.

The Haka is an item that comes in multiple forms but the most common in kapa haka is the ‘Haka Taparahi’, or a haka delivered in a set formation e.g. 2 rows. In kapa haka competitions it is primarily performed by the tāne with the wāhine supporting in specific ways to raise the level of the performance. It is said that the whole body must talk in haka and so it contains vigorous movements, gestures, to support the words. Haka are often performed during important moments, milestones, successes (like a graduation), weddings or tangihanga (funerals). It’s when a group gathers to perform in unison to showcase their mana, or respect.

Tāmaki Makaurau is the hosting rohe for Te Matatini National Kapa Haka Festival in 2023 and it will be held from 22-25 February at Ngā Ana Wai (Eden Park). 
Read more about the prestigious festival and the University's partnership at: https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2022/09/12/university-steps-up-to-matatini-partnership.html
