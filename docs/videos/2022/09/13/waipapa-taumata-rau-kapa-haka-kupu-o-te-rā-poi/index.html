This video is part of a series celebrating our partnership with Te Matatini National Kapa Haka Festival. Kaiārataki o te tari o PVC Māori, Michael Steedman explains the seven categories that all groups compete in.

Poi have been a part of Māori culture for centuries and at one time were made with raupō balls tethered with harakeke binding and string. Nowadays you’re able to make Poi out of softer materials. The Poi was used to strengthen and improve flexibility in the wrist, however now in kapa haka and at Te Matatini it’s about rhythm, skill, grace, speed and performed and led by wāhine Māori.

Tāmaki Makaurau is the hosting rohe for Te Matatini National Kapa Haka Festival in 2023 and it will be held from 22-25 February at Ngā Ana Wai (Eden Park). 
Read more about the prestigious festival and the University's partnership at: https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2022/09/12/university-steps-up-to-matatini-partnership.html
