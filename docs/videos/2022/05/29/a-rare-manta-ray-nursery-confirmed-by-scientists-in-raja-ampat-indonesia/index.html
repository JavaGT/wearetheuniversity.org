A rare manta ray nursery confirmed by scientists in Raja Ampat, Indonesia [59]

Description

University of Auckland PhD student, Edy Setyawan, led eight years of research that conclusively confirmed the existence of a nursery for manta rays in Wayag lagoon, Raja Ampat, eastern Indonesia.

Using a multidisciplinary approach, including satellite and acoustic tracking, the scientists provide the most robust assessment to date on manta ray nursery. Further details on the behaviours and residency of the juvenile reef manta rays in the nursery can be found in an article published in Frontiers in Marine Science: https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fmars.2022.815094/full.
