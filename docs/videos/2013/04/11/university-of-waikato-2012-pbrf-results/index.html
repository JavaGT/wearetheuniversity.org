Description

Today the government released its 2012 quality evaluation for performance-based research funding (PBRF). More than half of Waikato University's research-active academic staff have been rated in the top two categories, and the University is also among the top four New Zealand universities in having recognised researchers available for teaching and supervising postgraduate students.

University of Waikato Deputy Vice-Chancellor, Professor Alister Jones, speaks about the performance-based research funding and what this means to University of Waikato students, staff and stakeholders.

For more information about PBRF, please see http://bit.ly/ZNQPPm.
