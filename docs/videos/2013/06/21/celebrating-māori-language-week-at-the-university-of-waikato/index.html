Description

Māori Language Week is in its 39th year and the University of Waikato is taking every opportunity to celebrate it on campus. It runs from 1 July - 7 July and the theme for 2013 is Ngā ingoa Māori - Māori names.

Through the years, te reo has increasingly played a large role in University life says Pro Vice-Chancellor Māori Professor Linda Smith. "Our aim at this university is to provide a distinctive contribution to the University of Waikato experience for both staff and students, which draws on kaupapa Māori, the heritage of our region and our relationships with Waikato-Tainui and the different Iwi of our staff, students and regions. Te Wiki o Te Reo Māori is an opportunity for us at this university to celebrate our distinctiveness, our heritage and most of all the language of te reo."

In this video University of Waikato lecturers, Hine-iti-moana Greensill and Jackie Tuaupiki encourage viewers to get involved with Māori Language Week by providing some basic pronunciation tips around the Māori language and Waikato place names.
