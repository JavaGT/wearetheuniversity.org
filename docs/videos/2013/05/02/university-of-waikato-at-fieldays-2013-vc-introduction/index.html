Description

The University of Waikato is welcoming the opportunity to once again show off its innovative and groundbreaking agricultural research at the Southern Hemisphere's largest agricultural showpiece - the 2013 National Agricultural Fieldays.

From using microbes to remove excess nitrogen from land to transforming agricultural waste into a valuable commodity, University of Waikato researchers are at the leading edge of agricultural research, with many aspects of that research going on show at the National Agricultural Fieldays, which run at Mystery Creek from 12-15 June.

University of Waikato Vice-Chancellor, Professor Roy Crawford, explains why the Fieldays is such an important event for the university.

For more information about the university's involvement with Fieldays 2013, please see http://bit.ly/On756R.
