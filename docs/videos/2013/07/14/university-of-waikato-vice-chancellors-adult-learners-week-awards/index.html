Description

Adult Learners' Week aims to celebrate the efforts, achievements and contributions of adult learners, educators and providers, strengthen adult and community education, raise awareness of and widen access to learning opportunities for adults, encourage more people to seek advice and guidance about returning to learn, and foster collaborative and cooperative networks and pathways among those who share an interest in adult learning.

The University of Waikato has more than 4,800 adult learners, defined as students over the age of 25, making up 38% of the student body. To recognise the significant contribution adult learners make to Waikato, the University holds its own activities and celebrations during Adult Learners' Week with events organised by the Adult Learners' Week Committee and the Waikato Student Union.

For more information about the Vice-Chancellor's Adult Learners' Week Awards, please see http://bit.ly/QdLsrT.
