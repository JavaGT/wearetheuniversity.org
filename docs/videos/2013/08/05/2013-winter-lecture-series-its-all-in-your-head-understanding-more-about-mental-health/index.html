Description

Mental illness is nothing new, but treatments and understandings have changed over the years and we're now much more open about discussing depression and other conditions, including traumatic brain injuries -- sometimes called the "invisible epidemic".

Come along and hear Associate Professor Catharine Coleborne outline the history of insanity and institutions in the colonial world. WORLD fashion co-founder and CEO Denise L'Estrange-Corbet will talk about living with depression and Dr Nicola Starkey from Psychology will talk about her research in understanding the effects of TBI, perhaps caused by car accidents, sports, assaults and falls, on the developing brains of children and young people. This free public lecture takes place on August 14 at the University of Waikato's Gallagher Academy of Performing Arts.

The 2013 Winter Lecture Series runs each Wednesday evening throughout August, 6-7pm. For more information please see http://bit.ly/10VuBQA.
