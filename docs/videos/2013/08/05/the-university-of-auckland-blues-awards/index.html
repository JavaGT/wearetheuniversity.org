Description

The Blues Awards are part of a tradition from England. The blue colours of Oxford and Cambridge Universities came to symbolise sporting excellence. The earliest recorded Blue in New Zealand was awarded in 1922 to H.J. Wiley for Athletics. The tradition has been firmly embraced since 1930.
While a Blues Award has traditionally been a sporting accolade, there are now three categories of the award at The University of Auckland:
- Sports
- Arts
- Service and Leadership

Find out more: http://www.auckland.ac.nz/uoa/blues-awards
