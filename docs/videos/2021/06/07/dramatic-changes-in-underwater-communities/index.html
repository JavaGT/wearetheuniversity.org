Dramatic changes in underwater communities [1:51]

Watch on Youtube

Te Herenga Waka—Victoria University of Wellington

Description

Lough Hyne is Ireland’s only marine reserve. Over the last decade scientists have witnessed a massive shift in the lough’s biodiversity. Given this sea lough mimics deeper ocean environments, the human and natural impacts revealed will help us better understand our oceans and what we can do to save them.

This work is a collaboration between scientists in Ireland, Northern Ireland, the UK and New Zealand, led by Professor James Bell from Te Herenga Waka—Victoria University of Wellington, and in collaboration with the National Parks and Wildlife Service who are responsible for managing Lough Hyne.
