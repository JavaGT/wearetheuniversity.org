Description

A group of staff and students from the University of Otago encountered an entangled humpback whale off the Otago coast on Thursday afternoon (28th March 2019). The group was on a postgraduate field trip making observations of seabirds and marine mammals when they spotted the whale approximately 10 km east of Karitane. The whale had rope wrapped around its body and tail and was trailing a buoy which ap0peared to be from a fishing vessel.

They reported the sighting to the Department of Conservation (DOC), who are calling for any sightings of the whale in the hope a disentanglement team can get to the whale. The public are adivsed by DOC to NOT attempt to disentangle the whale as this can be dangerous and make it harder for the disentanglement team.
