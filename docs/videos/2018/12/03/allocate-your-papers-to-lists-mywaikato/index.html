Before choosing your papers in MyWaikato,
make sure you understand which papers you need to take in the year ahead.
Check the planner for your qualification on the University website.
Once you've decided on your papers, log into MyWaikato
and go to 'Qualifications and Papers'.
Select 'Complete enrolment'.
Work through each of the steps in order. Choosing your papers is the last step.
Paper selection works best on a desktop or laptop computer using Google Chrome.
Remember those different groups of papers in your degree planner?
They are here in your Paper Selection screen. Let's start with the first list.
These papers are compulsory
so you will need to take all of them over the course of your qualification.
Choose the papers you want from the list for this year by clicking 'Add'.
Those papers will show under 'Selected papers'.
You can remove papers using the rubbish bin icon.
Once you're done with that list, move down to the next,
read the instructions and then add the papers you want.
You may not need all of your lists this year.
Some might contain papers you've already done, or papers you can't do yet.
Some papers are listed more than once,
so make sure you choose the right location and the semester your prefer.
When it comes to electives, you'll see a search box.
Type your chosen paper code or part of its title in the search box.
Some papers might prompt a message saying you can't take that paper.
If you're not sure about the reason for this, contact the Student Centre.
If you still want to take that paper, you may need to apply for special permission.
Once you've chosen all your papers, take a look at your 'Selected papers' on the right.
A full-time workload is 60 points per semester and 120 points per year.
Now click 'View timetable'. This shows how youre timetable will look during the year.
Red means there's a clash and you should look at swapping your papers.
But if everything is looking good, click 'Done' and 'Next'.
You've now submitted your paper choices to the University for consideration.
We will send you an Enrolment Agreement.
When this arrives, log into MyWaikato and accept your Enrolment Agreement
to complete your enrolment.
If you need help choosing papers or using MyWaikato, contact our Student Centre Team.
