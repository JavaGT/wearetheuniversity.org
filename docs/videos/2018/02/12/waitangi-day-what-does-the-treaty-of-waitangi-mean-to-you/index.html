Description

Waitangi Day is recognised as New Zealand's national day. The date is an important marker in our country's history and recognises the significance of the Treaty of Waitangi as the nation's founding document.

Since the 1970s there has been increased debate surrounding the place of the Treaty in modern New Zealand. What does the Treaty mean to you?
