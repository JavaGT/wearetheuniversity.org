Description

This entertaining event gives thesis students the chance to present their research to a large audience while competing for a number of generous prizes.

Congratulations to Andrew Filmer from the Department of Music, whose talk entitled ‘String Retunings: Modern Approaches to a Lost Art’ earned him the Supreme Award. Andrew received a NZ$1500 study grant, and airfares and accommodation to represent Otago at the Australasian Three Minute Thesis Competition at the University of Queensland in Brisbane, which was held on 11 October 2012.

Second place and a NZ$750 study grant went to Allamanda Faatoese from the Department of Medicine at the University of Otago’s Christchurch campus, whose topic was ‘Why are Māori dying earlier of heart disease?’

Hemakumar Devan from the School of Physiotherapy was awarded third place and a NZ$500 study grant for his presentation ‘Amputation, Asymmetry and Low Back Pain.’ Hemakumar also won the NZ$250 prize for best presenter as voted by Dunedin high school students who were invited to attend the final.

Our other finalists were:

- Tim Crawford – Departments of Biochemistry and Botany
- Rebecca Cooke – Departments of Human Nutrition and Medicine
- Rebecca Van Amber – Department of Applied Sciences
- Neil Vallelly – Department of English and Linguistics
- Wiebke Finkler – Department of Tourism
