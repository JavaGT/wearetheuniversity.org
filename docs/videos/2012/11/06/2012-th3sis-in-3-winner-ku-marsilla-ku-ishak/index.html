Description

A chemical engineering student who's looking for ways to use blood polymers to make bioplastics was a double winner at the University of Waikato's Th3sis in 3 competition.

Ten doctoral students each had three minutes and a single static powerpoint slide to talk about their research and why it was important, with their presentations judged by a panel of experts and a full theatre at the Academy of Performing Arts.

We speak to 2012 Th3sis in 3 winner, Ku Marsilla Ku Ishak, about her PhD topic and what she plans to do with her $5,000 prize.

For more information about Postgraduate Month and the Th3sis in 3 competition, please see http://bit.ly/rgOGZE.
