Description

From researching the toxins in sea slugs in the South Island of New Zealand, to working with electronic giants Toshiba in Tokyo, or studying bird life in Tahiti, work placements at the University of Waikato in New Zealand are exciting and varied. Work placements are completed during a Bachelor of Engineering and a Bachelor of Science (Technology). The work placements are a compulsory part of the degree which involves the student working full-time within a company for 3-12 months, while earning course credit and being paid by the company. The University has a team of staff dedicated to helping you to find the best work placement for your interests. Many students are offered full-time work at the company on completion of their work placement. Students graduate with valuable workplace experience and great industry contacts.

For more information about work placements at The University of Waikato, please see http://bit.ly/OhlnGS.
