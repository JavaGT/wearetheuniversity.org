Description

In the 2008/2009 austral summer, a specialised time-lapse camera was deployed in the Miers Valley of Antarctica by the nzTABS field team from the University of Waikato.

The camera took photos every two hours during the following year, even in the total darkness of the Antarctic winter, when air temperature dropped below -40 degrees.

The photographs are used to estimate snow coverage patterns within the Miers Valley and to understand how snow may impact the Dry Valleys ecosystem. It allowed us to produce a movie documenting the events in a Dry Valley over the course of a year.

Enjoy and keep an eye out for: Antarctic moonrise, aurora australis, snow storms and lost graduate students.
