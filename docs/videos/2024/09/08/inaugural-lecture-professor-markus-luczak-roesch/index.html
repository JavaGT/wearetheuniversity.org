Complexity beyond pure reason: A quest to understand universal laws of change

Change happens everywhere and all the time—in biological systems, social systems, the Earth system, even in everyday situations. Sometimes we have the ability to anticipate change, and sometimes change surprises us, because the events causing it were not statistically likely to occur.

In this lecture Professor Markus Luczak-Roesch explains the complexity that gets revealed when we assume that everything is information, and how that may help us to understand change. By combining the lens of complexity science with the autobiographical account of a first-generation university student he discusses the following questions: What is information? How much information is too much information? And what does it mean for others that it was statistically unlikely for him to become a professor?

Te Maheno Chair in Complexity Science: https://www.wgtn.ac.nz/complexity-science
