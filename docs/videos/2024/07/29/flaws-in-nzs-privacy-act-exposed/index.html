Description

In his paper, 'Data Sovereignty and Privacy: A Chimera or Realistic Prospect in Aotearoa New Zealand?' Associate Professor Gehan Gunasekara examines New Zealand's approach to data sovereignty and highlights flaws in the Privacy Act 2020, particularly regarding accountability and the use of anonymised data. Gunasekara calls for legislative reforms, including encrypted data storage by local trustees to better protect Kiwis' personal information and modernise privacy regulations.
