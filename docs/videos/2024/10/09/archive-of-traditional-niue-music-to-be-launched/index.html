Archive of traditional Niue music to be launched [1:58]

Description

Waipapa Taumata Rau, University of Auckland will launch the Richard Moyle Survey of traditional music of Niue. The audio and visual recordings were made during the Constitution celebrations of the 10th anniversary of self-government, 40 years ago.

Former University of Auckland professor Richard Moyle, an ethnomusicologist expert, recorded the celebrations in both Niue and Aotearoa in 1984.

Huni Mancini, Archivist of Cultural Collections at the University says making the collection available to the public was a great way to highlight both Vagahau Niue (Niue Language Week) and it was also important to recognise the 50th celebration of Pule Fakamotu, 50 years of self-government.

“We hope the collection will benefit the Niuean community both locally and globally, and contribute towards the revitalisation of Vagahau Niue.”

The archive will be made available in a few months in January 2025.
