Ko te Tangata, i manaaki te whenua, ko te whenua i manaaki i te tangata.
For if we the people look after the land
the land shall look after us.
The Māori food and fiber sector is worth over
23 billion dollars and contributes over 2.3
billion to New Zealand's GDP which means
there's a need for Māori expertise, Māori
research, and Māori students within the
sector. The University of Waikato has
taken up the challenge to tackle the
future problems of Māori and agribusiness and
we have created a new programme. It is
called Tupu Rangatahi - Tupu Ahuwhenua. We're excited about delivering
this programme with the New Zealand
Institute of Primary Industry Management
and partners from Te Ao Māori and the agribusiness
world. The amazing thing about
studying agribusiness here at Te Raupapa,
the Waikato Management School is that we're
not only in the top 1% of Business
Schools globally, we're also in the heart
of the Agriculture and agritech sector
for New Zealand which gives students
amazing opportunities to connect into
the organisations they're going to go on
and support in their careers. This
three-year programme will teach agribusiness
and include specific topics
exploring agribusiness from a Māori
context and worldview. You'll benefit
from scholarships, special events, tailored
experience modules and internships which
will help prepare you to make an impact
in Aotearoa. Tupu Rangatahi - Tupu Ahuwhenua
will have a full wrap around service
for our Māori students - that includes
mentoring, that includes help with study,
that includes help with anything you
need here at the University, as well as
remember you can always submit all of
your assignments into te Reo Māori. Here at
the University of Waikato we have a deep
connection to Te Ao Māori.
Tupu Rangatahi - Tupu Ahuwhenua is more than just an academic
programme. You'll be exposed to a range of
other opportunities and experiences that
will enhance your ability to return into
the field of Māori in agribusiness.
Tupu Rangatahi Tupu Ahuwhenua opens February
2025, so e te iwi grab your pens, grab your
laptops and grab your gumboots, it's
time to apply.
