Description

Te Ara ki Angitū is an educational initiative to unite the University of Waikato with the two South Waikato township communities of Tokoroa and Putaruru.

This initiative comes after conversations between the Vice-Chancellor and the principals of the Tokoroa and Putaruru high schools, and the wider community, who identified a lack of access to the University because of affordability of courses, accommodation and personal transport. The premise is that the initiative will make the University of Waikato a strong, viable option for those who would not otherwise be able to participate. This initiative has been created in such a way so that this concept can be rolled out to other communities.

In terms of the name, ‘angitū’ is about potential, opportunity and success. In this context, it refers to the students in these schools who have always had potential and the aspiration, and who now have the means and the opportunity to be on the pathway of tertiary study.
