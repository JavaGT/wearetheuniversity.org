Description

Firstborn women are more likely to be overweight or obese than their second-born sisters. That’s according to a study by the Liggins Institute at the University of Auckland in New Zealand. Professor Wayne Cutfield explains to ONE News Reporter Will Hine that firstborns studied were nearly 30 per cent more likely to be overweight, and 40 per cent more likely to be obese than their second-born sisters. The latest findings back up the findings of three earlier studies by his team – in firstborn adult men and in children of both sexes.
