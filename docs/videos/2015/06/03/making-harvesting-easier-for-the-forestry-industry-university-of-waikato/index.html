Description

University of Waikato graduate student, Ben McGuinness, has devised a system that looks set to enhance forestry planting processes.

The PhD project features a system that automates the lifting and grading process of seedlings for forestry planting by using a robotic machine.

The idea for the yet-to-be-named lifter/grader came when treestock company ArborGen needed help with the lifting and grading process of their seedlings.

In this video we speak to Ben McGuinness, Associate Professor, Mike Duke, and ArborGen Manager, Nathan Milne, about the project and how it will benefit the forestry industry.

The lifter/grader will be on show at the University of Waikato's Premier Feature stand at the National Agricultural Fieldays at Mystery Creek, 10-13 June. For more information, please click here: http://bit.ly/On756R.
