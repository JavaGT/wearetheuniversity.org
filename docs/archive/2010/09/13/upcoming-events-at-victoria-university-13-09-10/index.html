Upcoming events at Victoria University 13/09/10

Monday, 13 September 2010, 5:25 pm
Press Release: Victoria University of Wellington

MEDIA ADVISORY

13 September 2010

Upcoming events at Victoria University

Below is a selection of seminars, courses, events and public lectures being run at Victoria University. These events are free unless specified otherwise and are open to the public. If you would like to interview any of the speakers please arrange through the contacts listed below.

Thursday, 16 September 2010

Criminology Symposium: Should we punish drug offenders?
Seminar/Talk
School of Social and Cultural Studies
Three respected speakers, Hon Simon Power, Ross Bell and Dr Chris Wilkins will be discussing the current social, political and legal responses to those who engage in illegal drug practices in New Zealand.
Time: 4pm
Venue: Hunter Council Chamber
Address: Level 2, Hunter Building Gate 1 or 2, Kelburn Parade
Contact: Criminology Symposium Ph (04) 463 5317
monica.lichti@vuw.ac.nz

Monday, 20 September 2010

Writers On Mondays
Performance
International Institute of Modern Letters
Short/Sharp/Script (1) at CIRCA: How much drama can you cram into 60 minutes? Find out as actors from the New Zealand College of Performing Arts perform rehearse readings of work produced by MA (Script) students at the IIML. This week David Geary introduces snappy dialogue from Eli Kent, Cruzanne Macalister, Hinekaa Mako, Sam Smith and Sonya Stewart.
Time: 12.15-1.15pm
Venue: Circa Theatre
Address: Cable St, Wellington
Contact: Katie Hardwick-Smith Ph (04)4636854
katie.hardwick-smith@vuw.ac.nz

Thursday, 23 September 2010

Writers On Mondays
Performance
International Institute of Modern Letters
Buddhist Rain: Join us for this special event to launch a new CD setting Bill Manhire’s poems to jazz. Norman Meehan, Colin Hemmingsen and Hannah Griffin provide the melodies and Bill Manhire reads the lyrics.
Time: 7pm
Venue: Soundings Theatre, Te Papa
Address: Cable St, Wellington
Contact: Katie Hardwick-Smith Ph (04)4636854
katie.hardwick-smith@vuw.ac.nz
