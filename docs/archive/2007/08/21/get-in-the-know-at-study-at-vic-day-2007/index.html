Get in the know at Study at Vic Day 2007

The population of Victoria University's Kelburn Campus will swell by more than 3,500 as students from throughout New Zealand come and sample life at the capital city university.

Study at Vic Day, held on Friday 24 August, offers students, their parents, whānau and teachers the chance to see what Victoria University has to offer as well as the chance to enjoy free lunchtime entertainment from fantastic Wellington band, OdESSA, in the Kelburn Campus Quad.

The lunchtime entertainment is just a small part of what will be happening during the day, which will be attended by about 3,500 visitors from Wellington and further afield.

Victoria’s Kelburn Campus will be the base of the day’s activities with more than 65 information sessions, as well as displays, competitions and the chance to win an iPod. Visitors will also have the opportunity to talk to staff and current students, look around the facilities and attend information sessions to find out what it is like to study the many subjects offered at Victoria for first-year students.

Tours of the Kelburn Campus, home of the Faculties of Humanities & Social Sciences, Science and Engineering, will be offered during the day – as well as tours of the Halls of Residence, the Faculty of Commerce & Administration and Faculty of Law (Pipitea Campus), the Faculty of Education (Karori Campus) and the Faculty of Architecture & Design (Te Aro Campus). Free buses will run between campuses during the day.
Photo opportunities:
8.30am-9.30am
About 3,500 prospective students will arrive at the Kelburn Campus Quad, to start the day with some fun competitions and activities. Student guides, wearing bright green polar fleece jackets, will be helping out. Radio station 98ZM will also be on hand.

9.30am-3.00pm
Vic Uni Expo will be on display in the Kirk Foyer and Overbridge. An additional Science Expo, with hands-on experiments, will be set up in the Maclaurin Foyer.

1pm-2pm
OdESSA will perform to the students in the Kelburn Campus Quad. Associate Professor Warwick Murray, who won a National Teaching Excellence Award in 2006, will be playing his own music. Students can also enjoy free cake and BBQ.
For more information on Study at Vic Day, contact Student Recruitment and Course Advice on 0800 VIC UNI (press 2), email us on: course-advice@vuw.ac.nz or visit http://www.victoria.ac.nz/srca/events/study-at-vic.aspx
