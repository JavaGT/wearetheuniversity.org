Advisory board to strengthen stakeholder relationships

Leading business and public sector representatives have put their weight behind Victoria University's Faculty of Commerce & Administration as members of its new advisory board.

Those joining the board of 20 members, chaired by John Shewan (Chairman of PriceWaterhouseCoopers NZ) include Sam Morgan (TradeMe CEO), Jane Diplock (Chairman of the NZ Securities Commission) and Garry Muriwai (CEO of the NZ Institute of Chartered Accountants).

Professor Peter Thirkell, Dean of the Faculty of Commerce & Administration, says the board's inaugural
meeting on 31 May represents the first step towards a deeper relationship between the Faculty and its influential external stakeholders.

"Victoria prides itself on its reputation as a leading provider of high-quality research-led business and economics education and expertise, nationally and in the Asia-Pacific region. The board will provide advice and assistance in maintaining this reputation, and in meeting our objectives to produce graduates ready to contribute their knowledge and skills to the success of the business and public sectors here and internationally," Professor Thirkell says.

"Its members will offer valuable external perspectives and, importantly, will further connect the Faculty with its stakeholders in the business, public and not-for-profit sectors."

Faculty strengths include the internationally-recognised Master of Public Policy, the Master of Applied Finance, and the Master of Business Administration – which celebrated its 21st year in 2005. The International Master of Business Administration is taught in Hong Kong in collaboration with the Chinese University of Hong Kong. Of the many qualifications on offer, the Bachelor of Commerce and Administration sees more than 1300 students graduate each year.

Last year the School of Accounting and Commercial Law gained accreditation to CPA Australia, the world's sixth largest professional accounting body, and the School has long held accreditation with the New Zealand Institute of Chartered Accountants. The Tourism programme in the Victoria Management School, which celebrated its 10th anniversary in 2006, received TedQual certification by the World Tourism Organisation.

The board will meet three times per year, with the inaugural meeting scheduled for 31 May.

The 20 members of the panel are:

John Albertson – CEO of the NZ Retailers’ Association
Rob Cameron – CEO and founder of Cameron Partners
Geoff Dangerfield – CEO of the Ministry of Economic Development
Jane Diplock – Chairman of the NZ Securities Commission
Ria Earp – CEO of the Mary Potter Hospice
Iain Fraser – CEO of Project Plus
Charles Finny – CEO of the Wellington Regional Chamber of Commerce
Tim Gibson – CEO of NZ Trade and Enterprise
Marise James – member of Landcorp Farming and FMG advisory boards
Margot Mains – CEO of Capital and Coast Health
Wendy McGuinness – CEO of Sustainable Future NZ
Sam Morgan – CEO and founder of TradeMe
Gary Muriwai – CEO of the NZ Institute of Chartered Accountants
George Reedy – Managing Director of Actrix NZ
John Shewan – Chairman of PriceWaterhouseCoopers NZ
Craig Sims – Managing Director of National Bank of NZ (Retail), ANZ Bank
Dr Peter Stevens – CEO of GS1 New Zealand
Ralph Stewart – CEO of AXA NZ
Katrina Troughton – CEO of IBM NZ
Peter Wolnizer – Dean of the Faculty of Economics and Business, University of Sydney
