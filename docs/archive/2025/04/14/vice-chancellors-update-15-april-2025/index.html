Vice-Chancellor's Update | 15 April 2025

undefined

Mon Apr 14 2025 12:00:00 GMT+1200 (New Zealand Standard Time)

Kia ora tātou

As we approach the Easter break, a reminder that the University is closed from Friday 18 April through to Easter Tuesday on 22 April which, as a University holiday, has been loaded into Hono for all staff. More information is available on the staff intranet here.https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/intranet/stay-informed/staff-communications/notices/easter-tuesday-leave-hono.html

Retirement of Adrienne Cleland After close to 20 years with the University, DVC Operations and Registrar, Adrienne Cleland, has announced her retirement. Adrienne will be with us until later in the year when it is expected a replacement will have been appointed. We will have an opportunity to celebrate Adrienne’s significant contribution to the University later in the year.

Changes to the University logo This week the University launches its refreshed logo. The new design brings together our traditional crest and our English and te reo Māori names into a simplified and contemporary design style. The launch follows extensive engagement with a wide range of our communities, including students, staff, alumni and mana whenua. Read more about the rationale and rollout of the revised logo, and its application, on the project pagehttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/intranet/stay-informed/improvement-projects-programmes/brand-evolution.html, News pageshttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2025/04/15/refreshed-university-logo-unifies-brand.html and Brand Guidelineshttps://styleguide.blogs.auckland.ac.nz/.

Amendments to the Education and Training Act Last week three amendments to the Education and Training Act 2020, including those related to the freedom of expression, were tabled in Parliament. You can read more about the proposed amendments herehttps://www.education.govt.nz/our-work/information-releases/issue-specific-information-releases/education-and-training-amendment-bill-no-2. The University has been asked to provide feedback on a range of questions to the Minister.

May I take this opportunity to wish you and your whānau a restful and peaceful Easter break.

Ngā mihi nui Dawn

Professor Dawn Freshwater Vice-Chancellor
