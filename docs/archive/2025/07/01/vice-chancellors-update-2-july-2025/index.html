Vice-Chancellor's Update | 2 July 2025

undefined

Tue Jul 01 2025 12:00:00 GMT+1200 (New Zealand Standard Time)

Kia ora tātou

I hope you all get some time to pause and recharge over the mid-semester break. As we move into the second half of the year, I would like to share some recent news from across our University community.

Selina Tusitala Marsh appointed inaugural Commonwealth Poet Laureate Professor Selina Tusitala Marsh has been appointed the inaugural Commonwealth Poet Laureate. This is an historic honour in the Commonwealth’s 75-year history, and a wonderful recognition of the power of the arts and humanities to connect and inspire us. I look forward to seeing all that Selina will bring to this important new role. Congratulations! Read more on our News pageshttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2025/07/01/selina-tusitala-marsh-appointed-inaugural-commonwealth-poet-laur.html.

Celebrating Research Excellence Awards Congratulations to the recipients of our 2025 Hīkina Kia Tutaki, Celebrating Research Excellence Awards, which recognise the outstanding contributions of senior and early-career researchers. I’d also like to acknowledge Professor Caroline Foster, Professor Melinda Webber and Professor Paul Donaldson, who received the University’s top honour, the Research Excellence Medal, for their exceptional work. A celebration will be held on 23 October to acknowledge these achievements. Read more about the winners on our News pageshttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2025/07/01/leading-academics-receive-top-research-award.html.

New short courses marketplace The University is launching a new short courses marketplace, UoA Short Courses, which will make it easier for prospective learners to find and register for our wide range of offerings in one place. This is an important step in supporting our strategy to be a leading provider of lifelong learning for working professionals. Find out more on our Notice pageshttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/intranet/stay-informed/staff-communications/notices/new-short-courses-marketplace-launching.html.

University Town Halls A reminder that this year’s Town Hall meetings are being held this week across our campuses. These sessions are not faculty-specific, and you’re welcome to attend whichever suits you best.

Wednesday 2 July 12.00pm-1.00pm | Newmarket Campus Atrium

2.30pm-3.30pm | City Campus Room B201-393

Thursday 3 July 11.00am-12.00pm | Tai Tonga Campus Room G02

Ngā mihi nui Dawn

Professor Dawn Freshwater Vice-Chancellor
