Vital school property in Maungakiekie given a boost

Monday, 21 February 2011, 1:29 pm
Press Release: New Zealand National Party

Peseta Sam Lotu-Iiga
MP for Maungakiekie
**21 February 2011 Media Statement
**

Vital school property in Maungakiekie has been given a $2,169,257.43 boost says Peseta Sam Lotu-Iiga MP for Maungakiekie.

“The Government’s Jobs and Growth package means we have new classrooms and a new non-teaching area and much needed building upgrades,” Peseta Sam Lotu-Iiga says.

“The Government has spent a total of $2,169,257.43 million upgrading our schools in Maungakiekie This is directly benefiting our students and gives our teachers modern and effective working environments.

“ I was able to see first-hand the considerable investment made in Stanhope Road School at the recent opening of its new facilities,” Peseta Sam Lotu-Iiga says.

“We are now spending $200 million a year more on school property than the previous government. This Government is committed to building a quality education system for our children, and I’m pleased we are seeing the benefits of this here in Maungakiekie”.

“Upgrading schools in Maungakiekie has also kept construction firms going at a time when other parts of the market have slowed down. Throughout the country, an additional 2000 jobs have been created because of this building programme.”

Jobs and Growth is the Government’s multi-billion dollar investment to fast-track important construction projects throughout New Zealand. Total spending on schools was $628 million last year and is expected to rise to $741 million over the next year.

Maungakiekie School upgrades:

Sylvia Park School $108,810.00 New Classroom
Onehunga Primary School $108,810.00 New Classroom
Panmure District School $123,016.99 Modernisation
Ellerslie School $136,537.22 Modernisation
Royal Oak Intermediate School $318,301.22 Modernisation
Stanhope Road School $1,373,782.00 3x New Classrooms, 1 New non-teaching space
Total: $2,169,257.43
