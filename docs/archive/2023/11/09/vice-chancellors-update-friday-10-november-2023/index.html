Kia ora ,

I hope this email finds you well.

The University of Auckland has been working with NZ Police to investigate a potential security threat made against the University on social media last night.

The Police have now determined that there is no evidence to suggest the threat is real.

Students can feel safe coming onto campus today, and exams will proceed as planned. 

Te Papa Manaaki | Campus Care

I acknowledge the disruption and concern this incident may have caused to our University community. If you need support or guidance, Te Papa Manaaki | Campus Care is a free, safe and confidential service to support the health, wellbeing and safety of everyone at University.

You can contact Campus Care via the online form on the website and a Case Manager will be in contact with you as soon as possible. Submit a support request [https://protect-au.mimecast.com/s/5ChVCq71EMFwPqwXuZRSU_?domain=uoa.custhelp.com].

For more information, please visit Te Papa Manaaki | Campus Care [https://protect-au.mimecast.com/s/ZAjKCr81GMHJ0gJ2szsuRO?domain=uoa.custhelp.com].

Our Counselling team also is available today. To reach out to them, please contact 0800 698 427 / 0800 MY UHCS to make an appointment.

Other student support services

More information about the University's support services for students is available on the Student Support webpage [https://protect-au.mimecast.com/s/OLZvCvl1M9SVjQVAhz9V_W?domain=uoa.custhelp.com].

Please note that this incident is unrelated to the Employment Court hearing currently underway.

Ngā mihi,

Dawn Freshwater Ihorangi | Vice-Chancellor Waipapa Taumata Rau | University of Auckland
