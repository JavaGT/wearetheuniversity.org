E ngā mana, e ngā reo, e ngā mātāwaka. Tēnā koutou katoa i runga i te kaupapa whakahirahira o Te Wiki o te reo Māori. Welcome to Māori Language Week 2023.

We are celebrating this year’s theme, Kia Kaha Te Reo Maori: Making the Language Stronger, with a series of events and activities across the University. For resources and an overview of the events and activites for this week, please visit Te Wiki o Te Reo Māori [https://protect-au.mimecast.com/s/2fpmCBNqMRHpoynQIzqIkV?domain=uoa.custhelp.com].

We have also unveiled a stunning new pou, He Taumata Rau, to honour past, present and future rangatira. You can read more about it on He Taumata Rau: University unveils new pou [https://protect-au.mimecast.com/s/kNbKCD1vOJHNPyx2I5AeXB?domain=uoa.custhelp.com].

New role for Professor Pene Mathew

I am pleased to announce that Professor Pene Mathew, Dean of Law, has been appointed as Human Rights Commissioner and President of the ACT Human Rights Commission in the Australian Capital Territory. In this important and exciting role, Pene will be working with human rights in a practical, impactful way for the benefit of residents of the Territory, the first Australian jurisdiction to adopt a Human Rights Act.

Pene will start in her new position in late October 2023, at which time Professor Warren Swain will take on the role of Acting Dean of Law. I would like to acknowledge and thank Pene for her outstanding leadership of the Law Faculty over her tenure.
