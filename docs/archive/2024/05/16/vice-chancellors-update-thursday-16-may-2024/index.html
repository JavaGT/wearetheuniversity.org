Vice-Chancellor's Update | Thursday 16 May 2024

undefined

Thu May 16 2024 12:00:00 GMT+1200 (New Zealand Standard Time)

Kia ora koutou

It was inspiring to see almost 7,000 students graduate and celebrate their achievements with their whānau at the recent Autumn graduations. Congratulations to all our graduates and sincere thanks to the academic and professional staff who have supported those students throughout their journey at the University. Thank you also to those who made it possible to hold dedicated ceremonies for each faculty this year. You can read more about the graduates and their stories on the Graduation news pagehttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/list.html?tag=news:graduation.

University reviews meeting

Professor Sir Peter Gluckman held an online meeting this morning for all staff to discuss the role of the University Advisory Group (UAG) and the Science System Advisory Group (SSAG). The government established the two groups to provide advice on the future of the New Zealand university system and how to strengthen the science, innovation and technology system.

If you could not attend the session, you can view the All-staff forum: University review recordinghttps://www.staff.auckland.ac.nz/en/news-events-and-notices/vc-staff-forums/2024/university-review.html.

Prime Minister's Science Prize

Congratulations to Dr Samuel Mehr from the School of Psychology for winning the 2023 Te Puiaki Kaipūtaiao Maea, Prime Minister's MacDiarmid Emerging Scientist Prize. Learn more about Samuel’s https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2024/05/02/samuel-mehr-unravelling-musics-mysteries.html work on the cognitive science of how humans perceive and produce musichttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2024/05/02/samuel-mehr-unravelling-musics-mysteries.html.

University Town Halls, 8-12 July

This year's Town Hall staff meetings will take place from Monday 8 July to Friday 12 July across various campus locations. As in previous years, these open conversations are not faculty-specific, and you are free to attend whichever meeting is most convenient for you. The 12 July meeting will be online and will replace the Wednesday 31 July all-staff Zoom forum. I look forward to seeing you there. For more information on dates and venues, please visit the Ngā kaupapa nui | Events pagehttps://www.staff.auckland.ac.nz/en/news-events-and-notices/events.%E2%88%95maxItems%E2%88%9520%E2%88%95query%E2%88%95town%20hall%E2%88%95year%E2%88%952024%E2%88%95.html.

All-staff forum, Wednesday 29 May

Please mark your diaries for the next all-staff forum at 12.00pm on Wednesday 29 May. A calendar invitation will be sent shortly.
