Vice-Chancellor's Update | Monday 1 July 2024

undefined

Sun Jun 30 2024 12:00:00 GMT+1200 (New Zealand Standard Time)

Kia ora tātou

Mānawatia a Matariki. I hope you enjoyed a relaxing Matariki long weekend with whānau and friends. As part of the University’s Matariki celebrations, a stunning new artwork by alumna Lisa Reihana was unveiled on 26 June. Read about it on our News pages.https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2024/06/26/lisa-reihana-maramatanga-artwork-b201.html

Celebrating Research Excellence Congratulations to the 2024 recipients of our Celebrating Research Excellence Awards. This year’s accolades recognise senior and early-career researchers who have significantly contributed to their respective fields through innovative and high-quality work. Additionally, we have commended the outstanding achievements of doctoral candidates and presented five best thesis awards. You can read more about the 2024 winners on our News pageshttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2024/06/24/celebrating-research-excellence-awardwinners-2024.html.

Draft Freedom of Expression and Academic Freedom Policy Consultation Thank you to everyone who submitted ideas on the Draft Freedom of Expression and Academic Freedom Policy consultation, which ended on Friday. We have received substantial feedback, which the advisory group will now collate and review before submitting its recommendations to UEC and Senate for review, and then to Council for approval.

APRU Annual Presidents’ Meeting Last week, the University welcomed university leaders across Asia-Pacific to Tāmaki Makaurau to address ocean sustainability challenges. It was a privilege to host the conference, facilitate collaboration amongst fellow research-intensive universities and collectively seek solutions to global challenges. Read more about the Annual Presidents’ Meeting on our News Pageshttps://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/news/2024/06/24/APRU-event-hosted-by-UOA-a-collaborative-thinktank.html. I would like to take this opportunity to thank all those who contributed to the success of this meeting, which included also hosting the APRU Women in Leadership Summit.

Reminder on Town Halls, 8-12 July This year’s Town Hall staff meetings start next week. You are free to attend whichever meeting is most convenient for you. Please note that the 12 July meeting will be online and replaces the Wednesday 31 July all-staff Zoom forum. Please visit the Ngā kaupapa nui | Events Pagehttps://www.staff.auckland.ac.nz/en/news-events-and-notices/events.%E2%88%95maxItems%E2%88%9520%E2%88%95query%E2%88%95town%20hall%E2%88%95year%E2%88%952024%E2%88%95.html for more information on dates and venues.

Ngā mihi nui Dawn

Professor Dawn Freshwater Vice-Chancellor
