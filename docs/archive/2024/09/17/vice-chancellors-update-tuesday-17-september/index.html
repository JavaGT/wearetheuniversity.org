Tēnā koutou katoa i runga i te kaupapa whakahirahira o Te Wiki o te reo Māori.

Welcome to Maori Language Week 2024. This year’s theme is ‘Ake Ake Ake – a forever language’, reminding us that te reo Māori is a resilient, adaptable and enduring language. The recently opened ReoSpace is hosting a series of events in celebration of the week, for more information please visit the ReoSpace page [https://uoa.custhelp.com/rd?1=AvN_~wpGDv8S~xb~Gv_U~yKClaoqdR37ufRmSz7~Pv_v&2=41488].

Endeavour Fund success

Congratulations to our academic researchers who have received more than $30 million in the 2024 Endeavour funding round, a testament to the innovative ideas and excellence of the University's research. You can read more about the fund and this year's recipients on our News pages [https://uoa.custhelp.com/rd?1=AvN_~wpGDv8S~xb~Gv_U~yKClaoqdR37ufRmSz7~Pv_v&2=41484].

Research and Innovation Office (RIO)

The Office of Research Strategy (ORSI) and teams in UniServices have come together to form a new entity, the Research and Innovation Office, Te Puna Tiketike. The intention is to ensure that all key research services sit together as a single, united team to support research excellence across the University, as we prepare for significant change in the public research landscape. You can read more about RIO on the website [https://uoa.custhelp.com/rd?1=AvN_~wpGDv8S~xb~Gv_U~yKClaoqdR37ufRmSz7~Pv_v&2=41490].

George Mason obituary

We were saddened to hear of the passing of major donor and alumnus George Mason ONZM last week. George's extraordinary 25 year giving contribution to the University included his generous provision of scholarships for Masters and doctoral students and a landmark $5 million donation to establish the George Mason Centre for the Natural Environment. He was recognised with an honorary doctorate in 2016. You can read more about George's life on our Obitituaries website [https://uoa.custhelp.com/rd?1=AvN_~wpGDv8S~xb~Gv_U~yKClaoqdR37ufRmSz7~Pv_v&2=41485].

Dr Andy Shenk retires

The Chief Executive of UniServices, Dr Andy Shenk, has announced his retirement after 12 years in the role. Andy's contribution to the growth and success of UniServices has been immense, and I know that you will join me in wishing him the very best in his retirement. You can read more about Andy on the UniServices website [https://uoa.custhelp.com/rd?1=AvN_~wpGDv8S~xb~Gv_U~yKClaoqdR37ufRmSz7~Pv_v&2=41486].
